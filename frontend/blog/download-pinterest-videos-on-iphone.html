How to Save Pinterest Videos to Your iPhone Camera Roll

By PinClip Editorial • Published March 2026 • 6 min read

For iPhone users, the iOS ecosystem is famously closed-off. While Android has historically allowed users to download files directly to their file managers with a high degree of freedom, navigating file downloads in iOS—especially media files that you want to see directly in your Camera Roll/Photos app—has traditionally been a bit of a headache.

When you are scrolling through Pinterest on your iPhone and stumble upon a beautiful interior design idea, a life-changing recipe, or an inspiring fitness routine, you might naturally look for a button to save the video. Pinterest allows you to save "Pins" to your virtual boards. However, the application stubbornly refuses to let you physically save the video file (the MP4) to your device storage.

If you've searched online for "how to download Pinterest videos on iPhone," you've probably encountered terrible advice. Tech blogs often recommend downloading untrustworthy "file manager" apps from the App Store filled with intrusive ads, or suggest "jailbreaking" your device.

The PinClip iOS Method

None of those risky workarounds are necessary. Thanks to improvements in iOS Safari (specifically since iOS 13), you can download files directly from the web and move them to your Camera Roll without any third-party app installations.

Using PinClip's Pinterest Video Downloader, retrieving content is secure, fast, and natively supported on your iPhone.

The Exact Process

1. Copy the Pinterest Link:

Open the Pinterest application on your iPhone. Locate the video or Idea Pin you wish to save. Tap the Share icon (usually located in the bottom right corner of the pin interface). A menu will slide up. Tap the gray button labeled Copy Link.

2. Open Safari:

Exit Pinterest and open the built-in Safari browser. In the URL bar, go to pinclip.com (or your current PinClip domain). Do not use an in-app browser like the one embedded in Facebook or Instagram for this; standard Safari is required for file management.

3. Use the Tool:

Paste your copied URL into the search box prominently displayed on our homepage. Tap the Download button. Wait approximately 1–3 seconds for our servers to fetch the video data. Once the page refreshes, a thumbnail of your chosen video will appear, accompanied by a Download MP4 button.

4. Initiate the Download:

Tap the Download MP4 button. Safari will ask you, "Do you want to download 'pinterest_video.mp4'?" Tap the blue Download text to confirm. If you look at the top left (or bottom left, depending on your Safari settings) of your browser screen, you will see a small circle with a downward-pointing arrow filling up. This is your download manager.

5. Move to Camera Roll:

This is the critical step most people miss. At this point, the video is saved in your iPhone's "Files" app, not your Camera Roll.

  • Tap the downward-pointing arrow icon in Safari.
  • Tap Downloads to open the list of downloaded files.
  • Tap on the video you just downloaded to preview it.
  • Tap the Share icon (a square with an upward arrow) located in the bottom left corner.
  • Scroll down the options list and select Save Video.

Conclusion

Congratulations! You have successfully bypassed the Pinterest restrictions and saved the video offline. Open your native Photos app, and you will see the full HD, watermark-free MP4 ready to be watched, edited, or sent via iMessage.
