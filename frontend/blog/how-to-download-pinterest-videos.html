Pinterest has evolved far beyond a simple image-pinning platform. But here's the frustration: Pinterest doesn't let you download videos directly. You can save pins to your boards within the app, but that only works when you have an internet connection. Why Download Pinterest Videos Locally?

Before diving into the how-to, let's address why you might want to save Pinterest videos in the first place. There are several practical reasons:

  • Offline access: Watch cooking tutorials in the kitchen without worrying about internet connectivity, or follow exercise routines at the gym where Wi-Fi might be spotty.
  • Content preservation: Digital content is ephemeral. Creators regularly delete old posts, rebrand their accounts, or let their profiles go dormant. If you rely on a specific tutorial for a project, saving it locally ensures you always have access.
  • Easy sharing: Not everyone has Pinterest. Downloading a video as MP4 lets you share it directly via WhatsApp, iMessage, Telegram, or email with friends and family who don't use the platform.
  • Personal collections: Build organized, offline libraries of recipes, DIY instructions, workout plans, or design inspiration that you can access anytime.
  • Presentations and projects: Students and professionals sometimes need to reference visual content in projects, reports, or presentations where embedding a live Pinterest link isn't practical.

Step 1: Find and Copy the Video Link

Open the Pinterest app on your phone, or visit pinterest.com on your desktop browser. Navigate to the video pin you want to download. This can be a standard video pin, an Idea Pin, or a Pinterest Reel.

On mobile: Tap the three-dot menu icon (⋯) or the share button (usually an arrow icon) on the pin. Select "Copy Link" from the sharing options.

On desktop: Click on the pin to open it in full view. Copy the URL directly from your browser's address bar. The URL will look something like https://www.pinterest.com/pin/123456789/.
