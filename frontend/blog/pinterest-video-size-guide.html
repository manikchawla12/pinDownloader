Pinterest Native Aspect Ratios

Unlike YouTube, which primarily uses a 16:9 widescreen format, Pinterest is built for vertical scrolling on mobile devices. Because of this, video aspect ratios are significantly different.

  • Vertical (9:16) - The Standard: This is the dominant format on Pinterest. It perfectly fills a smartphone screen. Whenever you download Pinterest Reels or Idea Pins, you are almost always downloading a 9:16 aspect ratio video (typically 1080 pixels wide by 1920 pixels tall).
  • Square (1:1): Often used for product showcases and recipe clips, square videos are less common today but are supported up to 1080x1080 resolution.
  • Standard (2:3 or 4:5): A mildly vertical orientation that performs well within Pinterest's grid layout without immediately taking over the user's entire display real estate.

File Size and Compression Limits

Creators uploading content to Pinterest face specific constraints. The maximum file size for a video pin upload is officially 2GB, with a maximum length of 15 minutes. However, the vast majority of videos range from 15 seconds to 1 minute, usually resulting in a file size of 5MB to 30MB.

Understanding Resolutions (720p vs 1080p)

When you click download, you want the best visual quality.

Pinterest typically transcodes creator uploads into multiple resolution streams to serve users with different internet speeds. Our backend is specifically programmed to ignore the low-bandwidth streams (like 360p or 480p) and extract the "highest stream" available.

Usually, this means you will be downloading a crisp 1080p file (1080x1920 pixels). If the original creator uploaded a lower quality video (like 720p), PinClip will simply fetch that maximum available resolution.
