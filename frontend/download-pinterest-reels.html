Everything You Need to Know About Pinterest Reels

Pinterest has heavily invested in short-form video content over the past few years, launching features like Idea Pins and more recently Pinterest Reels. These vertical, short-form videos are designed for quick consumption and are packed with condensed, actionable content — from 30-second makeup tutorials and rapid recipe demonstrations to fashion styling tips and home organization hacks.

Unlike standard Pinterest video pins, Reels and Idea Pins are specifically optimized for mobile viewing and are often shot in vertical (9:16) format. They've become one of the fastest-growing content types on the platform, with Pinterest reporting that engagement on short-form video content has increased significantly throughout 2025 and into 2026.

Reels vs. Idea Pins — What's the Difference?

Idea Pins were Pinterest's initial foray into stories-like content. They can contain multiple pages with a mix of video clips, images, text overlays, and interactive elements. Originally, Idea Pins couldn't receive outbound links, making them a pure engagement format.

Pinterest Reels represent the platform's newer approach to short-form video, more closely aligned with Instagram Reels and TikTok's format. They typically feature a single video clip with engaging transitions, music, and text overlays.
